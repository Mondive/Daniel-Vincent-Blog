How to Learn Anything: Fast learning technique

We all know that studying is one thing, but remembering what we read is a whole other thing entirely.

We work so hard to study, overcoming the temptation from our android devices... that movie that just came out. And three days after studying successfully the worst happens, we forget!

So how do we learn to retain information? Dr Timothy explains how to make recalling what you've read much more possible.

By using FAST as an acronym, Dr Timothy helps us understand the secret to becoming a master at recalling or remembering things easily.

Let's get right into it!

FAST: The Untold Truth

FAST as Dr Tim puts it, is an acronym for Forget, Active, State and Teach.

Now hold on one minute! we're trying to learn how to remember not how to forget!. Don't worry, hopefully you'll understand where his getting at.

1) Forget

What Dr Tim meant by forget is not to throw away what you've read, but to do away with what we think we know of what we are about to read.

He went further to create an example;

"The cell you were taught in secondary school isn't the same as the cell you're gonna learn about in college. Of course it's the same concept, but one is obviously gonna be more in-depth than the other."

So discard your previous knowledge on the topic and make room for new knowledge.

He also went on to indicate that we are also to forget our limitations.

"You don't become great by believing you're not. You become great by believing you truly are great!" - Daniel Vincent.

2) Active

To be able to recall more you'll need to be active, meaning you need to keep revising what you've learnt. Reread it a hundred times if you have to. You didn't learn your alphabet by reading it once so why should now be any different!?

3) State

Your emotional and mental state plays a crucial role in determining if you're going to study well. You can be with your book all day, but if you're not in the right state of mind, you're pretty much better off staring at a brick wall. Nothing will get through.

Trust me, I've been there...

Information × emotions = long term memory

And lastly, Teach

We've all heard that teaching makes you learn better, yes, it does. It actually makes you learn twice as much better!.

Whatever you teach, you get to learn it twice.

Overall, following this four steps is without doubt going to ensure your success in any exam. So go forth and share this article quicken your pace. Time waits for no one.
